# Staff Briefing Guide - Radio Check

## Your Role in Safeguarding

As a staff member (counsellor or peer supporter), you play a critical role in keeping users safe. This guide explains what you need to do.

---

## 1. Acknowledging Safeguarding Alerts

### What Are Alerts?

When the AI detects a user may be in distress, a **Safeguarding Alert** appears on your dashboard. These are colour-coded:

| Colour | Meaning | Your Response Time |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| 🔴 **RED** | Imminent risk - user may be in crisis | **< 5 minutes** |
| 🟠 **AMBER** | High risk - significant distress | **< 15 minutes** |
| 🟡 **YELLOW** | Medium risk - needs attention | **< 30 minutes** |
| 🟢 **GREEN** | Low risk - monitoring only | Review when available |

### How to Acknowledge

1. **See the alert** on your dashboard under "Safeguarding Alerts"
2. **Click "Acknowledge"** button on the alert card
3. **Take action** - Chat or Call the user (buttons on the card)
4. **Document** what happened (use Report Incident if significant)

### Critical Points

- ⚠️ **Never ignore a RED alert** - even if you're busy, acknowledge it
- 📱 **Multiple staff see alerts** - if you can't handle it, someone else will
- 📝 **All acknowledgements are logged** - for governance tracking

## 2. When to Report an Incident

### Always Report

| Situation | Incident Level |
|-----------|---------------|
| User expressed suicidal thoughts | Level 2 - High |
| You contacted emergency services (999) | Level 3 - Critical |
| External safeguarding referral made | Level 3 - Critical |
| User disclosed abuse | Level 3 - Critical |
| System was down when user needed help | Level 2 - High |
| You handled a crisis call successfully | Level 1 - Moderate |
| Near-miss (something almost went wrong) | Level 1 - Moderate |

### How to Report

1. **Click "Report Incident"** button on your dashboard (under Quick Actions)
2. **Fill in the form:**
   - Title: Brief description
   - Level: Select severity
   - Description: What happened, who was involved, what action taken
   - Session ID: If you have it (from the chat/alert)
3. **Submit** - Admin and CSO will review

### Don't Wait

Report incidents **as soon as possible** after they happen, while details are fresh.

---

## 3. Response Time Targets

These are the targets we're measured against:

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| High-risk alert acknowledgement | **< 15 minutes** | User may be in crisis |
| Imminent risk first contact | **< 5 minutes** | Every minute counts |
| General alert acknowledgement | **< 30 minutes** | Shows we're monitoring |

### Tips for Meeting Targets

- ✅ Keep the Staff Portal open when you're on duty
- ✅ Enable sound notifications (bell icon in header)
- ✅ Check the dashboard regularly
- ✅ If you're stepping away, set yourself as "Unavailable"

---

## 4. Reporting Peer Concerns

If you observe concerning behaviour from another peer supporter:

### What to Report

- Harassment or bullying of users
- Encouraging self-harm
- Threatening behaviour
- Sharing inappropriate content
- Any safeguarding concerns

### How to Report

1. **Click "Report Peer Concern"** button on dashboard
2. **Identify the peer** (name or user ID)
3. **Select the reason** for your concern
4. **Describe what you observed**
5. **Submit** - Admin will investigate confidentially

### Confidentiality

Your report is **confidential**. The peer won't know who reported them.

## 5. Using the Staff Portal

### Dashboard Sections

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Safeguarding Alerts** | Users who may need immediate help |
| **Live Chats** | Active conversations |
| **Screening Results** | Users who completed assessments |
| **Active Callbacks** | Calls you're handling |
| **Quick Actions** | Report incidents/concerns |

### Key Buttons

- 🔄 **Refresh** - Update each section manually
- 📞 **Call** - Start a call with the user
- 💬 **Chat** - Open chat with the user
- ✅ **Acknowledge** - Mark that you've seen an alert
- ⚠️ **Report Incident** - Log something significant

---

## 6. Shift Handover

When your shift ends:

1. **Check for active chats** - Don't leave users mid-conversation
2. **Complete any pending acknowledgements**
3. **Report any incidents** from your shift
4. **Update your availability** status
