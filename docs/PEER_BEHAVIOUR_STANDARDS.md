# Peer Supporter Behaviour Standards - Radio Check

## Welcome to Radio Check

As a peer supporter in the Buddy Finder system, you're helping fellow veterans by sharing your experiences and offering support. This guide explains what's expected of you.

---

## The Golden Rules

### ✅ DO

| Behaviour | Why It Matters |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Be supportive and understanding** | You've been there too |
| **Listen without judgement** | Everyone's journey is different |
| **Share your own experiences** | That's why you're here |
| **Encourage professional help when needed** | Know your limits |
| **Report concerns about others** | Keep everyone safe |
| **Respect boundaries** | Yours and theirs |

### ❌ DON'T

| Behaviour | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Encourage self-harm or suicide** | Immediate ban |
| **Harass or bully other users** | Warning → Suspension → Ban |
| **Share harmful content** | Suspension or ban |
| **Threaten anyone** | Immediate suspension |
| **Try to exploit vulnerable users** | Immediate ban + referral |
| **Give medical/professional advice** | Warning |

---

## Communication Guidelines

### Healthy Peer Support

```
✅ "I understand - I felt like that too when I first came back"
✅ "Have you thought about talking to someone professional about that?"
✅ "What helped me was talking to Combat Stress"
✅ "You're not alone in feeling this way"
```

### Not Peer Support

```
❌ "You should just try harder"
❌ "I know exactly what medication you need"
❌ "Don't bother with the professionals, they don't understand"
❌ "Here's my personal phone number, call me anytime"
```

## When Someone Is In Crisis

If a fellow veteran tells you they're thinking about harming themselves:

### Step 1: Don't Panic
Your role is to listen, not to solve everything.

### Step 2: Show You Care
```
"I'm really glad you told me that. It takes courage to share."
```

### Step 3: Encourage Professional Help
```
"Have you spoken to anyone about this? The AI team here can connect you with trained support."
```

### Step 4: Share Resources
- **Samaritans**: 116 123 (free, 24/7)
- **Veterans UK**: 0808 1914 218
- **Combat Stress**: 0800 138 1619
- **In emergency**: 999

### Step 5: Don't Try to Handle It Alone
If you're worried, message the Radio Check team through the app. **You won't get in trouble for asking for help.**

## Reporting Concerns

### When to Report Another Peer

Report if you see someone:
- Encouraging self-harm
- Bullying or harassing users
- Sending threatening messages
- Behaving inappropriately
- Anything that makes you uncomfortable

### How to Report
1. Use the **Report** button on their message or profile
2. Select the reason
3. Add details about what happened
4. Submit - it's confidential

### You Won't Be Named
Reports are confidential. The person won't know who reported them.

## What Happens If You Break the Rules?

| Offence | First Time | Second Time | Third Time |
|---------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Minor** (e.g., giving advice beyond your role) | Warning | Final warning | Suspension |
| **Moderate** (e.g., harassment) | Suspension | Ban | - |
| **Serious** (e.g., encouraging self-harm) | Immediate ban | - | - |

### Appeals
If you receive a warning or suspension, you can appeal by emailing admin@radiocheck.me.

## Looking After Yourself

### You Matter Too

Peer support can be emotionally heavy. Remember:

- ✅ **It's OK to step back** if a conversation is too much
- ✅ **You're not responsible** for fixing someone else's problems
- ✅ **Take breaks** when you need them
- ✅ **Talk to someone** if peer support is affecting you
- ✅ **Use the AI buddies** yourself if you need support

### Signs You Need a Break

- Feeling overwhelmed by other people's problems
- Bringing conversations home with you
- Difficulty sleeping after peer support
- Feeling responsible if someone is struggling

**If this is you, take a break. Your wellbeing matters.**
