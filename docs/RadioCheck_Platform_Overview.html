1. Executive Summary

Our Mission

Radio Check exists to ensure no veteran faces their mental health struggles alone. We provide immediate, accessible, 24/7 support through AI companions trained to understand military culture, backed by human peer supporters and professional counsellors.

2. The Problem We Solve

The Veteran Mental Health Crisis

Critical Statistics:
  • Approximately 2 veteran suicides per week in the UK
  • Veterans are 2-3x more likely to experience depression than civilians
  • 17% of veterans report PTSD symptoms
  • Only 50% of veterans with mental health issues seek help
  • Average delay between symptoms and seeking help: 10+ years

Barriers to Support

Barrier Impact How Radio Check Addresses It
Stigma Veterans fear appearing weak or "broken" Anonymous AI support available 24/7 without judgment
Waiting Times NHS mental health waiting lists can be months Immediate access to AI companions and peer support
Civilian Services "They don't understand military life" AI trained on military culture; peer supporters are veterans
Crisis Timing Mental health crises don't follow office hours 24/7 availability with automatic crisis escalation
Geography Rural veterans far from services Digital platform accessible anywhere with internet

Our Solution

Radio Check creates a layered support system that meets veterans where they are:

  1. Layer 1: AI Companions - Immediate, anonymous, always available
  2. Layer 2: Peer Supporters - Trained veterans who understand the experience
  3. Layer 3: Professional Counsellors - Qualified mental health professionals
  4. Layer 4: Crisis Services - Automatic escalation to emergency services when needed

5. AI Companion System

Overview

Radio Check's AI Companions (known as "Battle Buddies") are sophisticated conversational AI agents designed specifically for veteran mental health support. Each AI has a distinct personality and communication style, allowing users to find a companion that resonates with them.

5.1 AI Characters

Character Personality Best For Location in App
Tommy Direct, no-nonsense Army veteran Users who prefer straight-talking support Crisis Support Page
Rachel Warm, non-judgemental support companion for veterans in the criminal justice system Veterans in or leaving prison, facing charges, or dealing with criminal records Criminal Justice Page
Hugo Knowledgeable navigator Users needing help finding services Organisations Page
Margie Peer supporter personality Users wanting a mate to chat with General Support
Bob Practical problem-solver Users seeking practical advice General Support

5.2 Conversation Continuity

A key feature of Radio Check's AI system is conversation memory. When a user returns to chat with an AI companion, the system:

  • Loads the last 15 messages from previous conversations
  • Provides this context to the AI with instructions to continue naturally
  • Instructs the AI to reference specific previous topics ("How'd that thing go?")
  • Creates the feeling of talking to a mate who remembers you
Privacy Note: Conversation history is encrypted and stored locally on the user's device, not on our servers. Users can clear their history at any time.

5.3 AI Consent & Disclaimer

Before first use of AI companions, users must acknowledge:

  • AI companions are not human and cannot replace professional help
  • For emergencies, users should contact 999 or crisis services
  • Conversations may be reviewed for safety purposes
  • AI responses should not be considered medical advice
  • Voice-to-text dictation is available for easier input

This consent is requested once per app installation, not repeatedly per conversation.

6. Safeguarding & AI Safety

Zentrafuge AI Safety Architecture

Radio Check integrates Zentrafuge's AI safety modules - a research-led approach to building AI that earns trust rather than assumes it. This architecture ensures that AI interactions are continuously monitored for safety concerns.

6.1 Real-Time Safety Monitoring

Every message in AI conversations is analysed by our safety classifier system:

Risk Level Triggers Response
LOW General conversation, no risk indicators Normal conversation continues
MEDIUM Expressions of distress, hopelessness, mentions of self-harm AI provides supportive response with helpline information
HIGH Active suicidal ideation, immediate danger indicators Immediate crisis resources displayed, alert to staff
CRITICAL Explicit suicide plan, imminent self-harm, harm to others Staff notification, emergency services information, session flagged

6.2 Safety Classifier Technology

The safety system uses a multi-layered approach:

  1. Keyword Detection: Primary screening for high-risk phrases
  2. Semantic Analysis: Understanding context and intent using embeddings
  3. Sentiment Analysis: Detecting emotional tone and distress levels
  4. Pattern Recognition: Identifying escalating crisis patterns over time
  5. Human Review: High-risk conversations flagged for staff review

6.3 Safeguarding Alerts

When the safety system detects concerning content:

  • Alert generated with risk level, category, and timestamp
  • Alert appears in Staff Portal and Admin Portal dashboards
  • Assigned staff member notified
  • Full conversation transcript available for review
  • Actions logged for audit trail

Alert Categories Monitored

  • Suicidal ideation
  • Self-harm mentions
  • Violence towards others
  • Child safeguarding concerns
  • Domestic abuse indicators
  • Severe mental health crisis
  • Substance abuse crisis
  • Financial exploitation

For safeguarding alerts, staff can:

  • Mark alert as resolved with outcome

10. Compliance & Regulatory Framework

Radio Check is designed to meet or exceed all relevant regulatory requirements for mental health support services in the United Kingdom.

10.1 Care Act 2014 Compliance

Safeguarding Adults Requirements

  • Section 42: Local authority duty to make enquiries - Radio Check facilitates referral to local safeguarding teams when concerns are identified
  • Section 43: Safeguarding Adults Boards - Platform designed to integrate with local SAB procedures
  • Six Principles: Empowerment, Prevention, Proportionality, Protection, Partnership, Accountability - embedded in all safeguarding procedures

10.2 Mental Health Act Considerations

Mental Health Act 1983 (as amended 2007)

  • AI companions trained to recognise when users may require formal mental health assessment
  • Clear escalation pathways to Approved Mental Health Professionals (AMHPs)
  • Information provided about rights under the Mental Health Act
  • Crisis protocols aligned with Section 136 procedures

10.3 BACP Ethical Framework Alignment

British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy Standards

Peer supporter training aligns with BACP ethical principles:

  • Being Trustworthy: Confidentiality training, clear boundaries
  • Autonomy: Respecting user choices and self-determination
  • Beneficence: Acting in users' best interests
  • Non-maleficence: Avoiding harm, knowing limits
  • Justice: Fair treatment, anti-discrimination training
  • Self-respect: Self-care training for supporters

10.4 NHS Digital Standards

NHS Service Standard Alignment

  • DCB0129 Clinical Risk Management (software as medical device considerations)
  • DCB0160 Clinical Risk Management (health IT deployment)
  • NHS Digital Technology Assessment Criteria (DTAC) principles
  • Interoperability with NHS services (Op Courage, NHS 111)

11.2 Special Category Data

Mental health data is "special category data" under GDPR, requiring additional protections:

  • Explicit Consent: Users consent to processing before using AI companions
  • Vital Interests: Safeguarding actions may process data without consent where life is at risk
  • Healthcare Provision: Processing necessary for provision of health services
  • Public Interest: Safeguarding is a substantial public interest
